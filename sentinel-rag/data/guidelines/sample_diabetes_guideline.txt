Management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus in Adults (Test Document)

SECTION 1 — FIRST-LINE THERAPY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 Metformin is the recommended first-line pharmacological therapy for adults
with type 2 diabetes mellitus unless contraindicated. Initiate at 500 mg once
daily with the evening meal to reduce gastrointestinal intolerance.

1.2 Titrate the metformin dose gradually over 4 to 6 weeks, increasing by
500 mg per week as tolerated, up to a maximum of 2000 mg per day in divided
doses.

1.3 First-line therapy should always be combined with structured lifestyle
intervention, including medical nutrition therapy and at least 150 minutes of
moderate-intensity physical activity per week.

1.4 If the patient is unable to tolerate metformin, a second-line agent such as
a fictional-class SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., "Glucoflozin") or a DPP-4 inhibitor
(e.g., "Gliptamax") may be considered as initial therapy.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2 — BLOOD GLUCOSE TARGETS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 The general glycemic target for most non-pregnant adults is an HbA1c of
less than 7.0 percent (53 mmol/mol).

2.2 A more relaxed HbA1c target of less than 8.0 percent (64 mmol/mol) is
appropriate for older adults with multiple comorbidities, limited life
expectancy, or a history of severe hypoglycemia.

2.3 Recommended fasting plasma glucose target is 80 to 130 mg/dL
(4.4 to 7.2 mmol/L).

2.4 Recommended postprandial (2-hour) plasma glucose target is less than
180 mg/dL (10.0 mmol/L).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3 — MONITORING FREQUENCY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Measure HbA1c at least twice per year in patients who are meeting glycemic
targets and have stable glycemic control.

3.2 Measure HbA1c quarterly (every 3 months) in patients whose therapy has
changed or who are not meeting glycemic targets.

3.3 Patients on insulin or sulfonylureas should perform self-monitoring of
blood glucose at a frequency individualized to their regimen and hypoglycemia
risk.

3.4 Assess renal function (estimated glomerular filtration rate, eGFR) at least
annually in all patients on metformin, and more frequently if eGFR is below
60 mL/min/1.73m2.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4 — CONTRAINDICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Metformin is contraindicated in patients with an eGFR below
30 mL/min/1.73m2 due to the risk of lactic acidosis.

4.2 Metformin should be temporarily discontinued in patients undergoing
procedures requiring iodinated contrast media and resumed only after renal
function has been confirmed stable at 48 hours.

4.3 The fictional SGLT2 inhibitor "Glucoflozin" is contraindicated in patients
with a history of recurrent genital mycotic infections or volume depletion.

4.4 Sulfonylureas are not recommended as first-line therapy in older adults due
to an elevated risk of hypoglycemia.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5 — DRUG INTERACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Concurrent use of metformin with carbonic anhydrase inhibitors (e.g., the
fictional agent "Acetazol-X") may increase the risk of lactic acidosis; monitor
closely and consider dose reduction.

5.2 The fictional DPP-4 inhibitor "Gliptamax" should not be combined with the
fictional GLP-1 receptor agonist "Incretalide", as the combination provides no
additional benefit and increases gastrointestinal adverse effects.

5.3 Co-administration of SGLT2 inhibitors with loop diuretics increases the risk
of volume depletion and hypotension; monitor blood pressure and renal function.

5.4 Beta-blockers may mask the adrenergic warning symptoms of hypoglycemia in
patients treated with insulin or sulfonylureas; counsel patients accordingly.
